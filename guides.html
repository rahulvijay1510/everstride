How to wash and care for wool shoes

4 min read · Everstride team

One of the best things about our shoes is that keeping them fresh is genuinely easy. Wool naturally resists odor, and when a deeper clean is due, the washing machine has your back.

Machine washing, step by step

  • Remove the insoles and laces and wash them separately or by hand.
  • Use a gentle, cold cycle. Cold water protects the wool fibers and the shape.
  • Skip the dryer. Air dry at room temperature, away from direct heat, which can shrink or warp the materials.
  • Reshape while damp and let them dry fully before wearing again.

For quick touch-ups between washes, a damp cloth and a little mild soap handle most scuffs. Wool’s natural odor resistance means you will wash far less often than you might expect.

Choosing a material

Wool vs Terra: which Everstride material is right for you

4 min read · Everstride team

Our two signature materials feel different underfoot and suit different lives. Here is how to choose.

Choose Wool if

  • You want cozy, cushioned softness and a slightly warmer feel.
  • You live somewhere with real seasons; wool regulates temperature year round.
  • You love a classic, textured look.

Choose Terra if

  • You run warm or live somewhere hot and humid.
  • You want the breeziest, most breathable option for sockless summer wear.
  • You prefer a smoother, silkier surface.

Still unsure? Many people own both: wool for cooler months and tree for warmer ones. Both are machine washable and both carry a carbon footprint you can see on the box.

What a carbon footprint label on a shoe actually means

3 min read · Everstride team

We print a number like “7.1 kg CO2e” on every product. Here is what that means and why it matters.

CO2e stands for carbon dioxide equivalent. It adds up all the greenhouse gases created across a product’s life, from growing the materials to manufacturing, shipping, and eventually disposal, and expresses them as a single comparable number. A lower number means less climate impact.

Why we put it on the box

  • Transparency. You can compare the impact of what you buy, the way a nutrition label lets you compare foods.
  • Accountability. Publishing the number forces us to keep lowering it, not just talk about sustainability.
  • Context. For reference, an average pair of sneakers is estimated to generate substantially more CO2e than our lightest styles.

Reducing that number is the whole game: cleaner materials, renewable energy in manufacturing, and efficient shipping, with verified offsets for whatever remains.
