🍃 Our story

Better things, made in a better way

We started with a simple question: why are shoes, some of the most-worn objects on earth, made from materials that harm it? So we built ours from nature instead.

Everstride began with a New Zealander’s conviction that merino wool, a material his home country knows well, could make a radically more comfortable and sustainable shoe. Paired with a co-founder in renewables, that idea became a company built on a plain belief: comfort and sustainability should not be a trade-off.

Nature as our first material

Most footwear is made from synthetics derived from fossil fuels. We start somewhere else: soft merino wool, silky eucalyptus tree fiber, and a foam made from sugarcane. These renewable materials perform better against skin, breathe more, and carry a fraction of the carbon of petroleum-based alternatives.

The carbon footprint on every box

We think you should know the impact of what you buy, so we measure and print the carbon footprint of every product, the same way food labels show calories. It keeps us honest, and it drives us to bring that number down year after year through better materials, renewable energy, and smarter shipping.

  • Carbon neutral operations, with reductions first and verified offsets for the rest.
  • B Corp certified, meeting high standards of social and environmental performance.
  • Responsible wool and forestry, sourced to recognized welfare and sustainability standards.
  • Designed to last, because the most sustainable shoe is one you keep wearing.

Who we make them for

Our shoes are for people who want everyday comfort without ignoring where their things come from: commuters, travelers, parents, and anyone on their feet who would rather tread a little lighter. You do not have to choose between feeling good and doing good.

We are not perfect, and we do not pretend to be. But printing our footprint keeps us accountable, and every design choice is a chance to make the number smaller. That is the work.
